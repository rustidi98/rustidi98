## This move is personal

My name is Rustem. I am 28 and live in Kazan with my wife and our four-year-old daughter. We have travelled together and seen different places, but at this stage we are not looking for another temporary country or a short overseas experience. We want to find a place that can eventually become home.

For us, that means a calm and safe environment, good education for our daughter, a climate in which we can genuinely enjoy daily life and a culture where we can build relationships with local people rather than remain permanent outsiders. New Zealand is the country in which those priorities came together most clearly for us.

## Why I am moving through work

I plan to enter New Zealand through a real full-time role and build stability before or together with my family's relocation. I am not looking for a nominal employer or a visa arrangement detached from the work. I want to join a company whose problems I can genuinely help solve and become part of its team and professional community.

That is also why I am preparing my marketing and lead-generation agency for sale. The agency is an operating business serving more than 15 education companies and generating approximately 5,000 leads per month across its client portfolio, but I do not want an employer to wonder whether New Zealand is secondary to my existing work. The relocation and the new role are the commitment.

## What family changes about the way I choose a company

Moving alone for a short contract and moving with a wife and child are different decisions. I pay attention not only to the title or salary, but also to the people, the purpose of the product, the way the team treats responsibility and whether we could build a long-term relationship.

I value:

- finishing what I start rather than handing over an unfinished problem;
- making decisions independently and taking responsibility for their consequences;
- correcting my own mistakes instead of shifting responsibility to a colleague, customer, circumstance or tool;
- integrity, honesty and a clear separation between facts, estimates and assumptions;
- discipline, visible planning and an understandable definition of done;
- the ability to move through ambiguity without waiting for perfect instructions;
- leaving behind a system, decision trail or process that makes the next person's work easier;
- direct communication, listening and support when somebody is struggling.

These are professional principles, but they are also the qualities I want my daughter to see in the adults around her.

## What I hope to build in New Zealand

Professionally, I want to continue working where applied AI, full-stack engineering and real operating problems meet. I am strongest when I can get close to customers, understand an incompletely defined workflow, connect product and business logic and remain responsible through production use.

Personally, we want ordinary things that matter a great deal: a stable home, a school our daughter enjoys, family weekends, friends, nature and the feeling that we are participating in the place where we live rather than waiting for the next move.

This is why my New Zealand search is focused and long term. I require employer support for an Accredited Employer Work Visa, but the visa is the mechanism, not the purpose. The purpose is to build useful work and a life our family can commit to.
